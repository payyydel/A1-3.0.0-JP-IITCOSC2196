16 Personalities | Myers-Briggs



I believe self-awareness is an asset. A tool to be delicately wielded. These results simply articulate my inner workings and allow me the resources to make informed and healthy decisions. I don’t believe these results will have a particularly noticeable effect in a group context. A bolstered ego isn’t really in line with my personality type, nor does it play a part in my personal demeanour. The only impact I believe these results may have is a reaffirming one. The results are accurate because I place great value on a team that fosters equal opportunity and free-flowing ideas. Clear and empathetic communication towards a person's particular point of view is a must. Furthermore, conflict isn’t a particularly strong suit of mine; however, I believe that honesty is the best policy in a professional environment.

When forming a team, I believe in honesty upfront but genuine encouragement that doesn't make people bloated or overfed. Honesty when explaining my capabilities, life circumstances that may inadvertently impact my team, and its work. The results form a coherent and easy-to-understand means to express this to a group.



V.A.R.K Learning Test

The results are very actionable. Relatively speaking, definitively knowing more about my learning style allows for a more methodical approach. Or as methodical as I can manage. There's no point in trying to drastically change my learning style. Leaning into my strengths can only benefit my team and me. Obviously, I'd choose to share the results upfront. An informed team is a team ready to plan, and as lovely Ben summed it up, "if you fail to plan, you plan to fail". Considering how everyone else learns, dividing work reasonably, but playing to people's strengths. As well as collectively collaborating on tasks that push people's limits. I've found that remaining static and comfortable is a remarkably prolonged learning method and a surefire way to eventually regress.



Inductive and Deductive Reasoning Test

These results are not driven by any emotional or personality-based bias but instead by factual reasoning designed to be more accurately reflective of one’s innate and developed abilities surrounding inductive and deductive reasoning. Partnered with more personality-based results, these allow me to determine more objectively what I'm capable of on a more factual level. I believe they would provide an insightful and logical perspective during decision and problem-solving situations. Combined with varying different perspectives and strengths, I’ll be able to thoughtfully contribute to team discussions and workloads. While inductive and deductive reasoning inherently helps make decisions based on factual evidence surrounding an individual's present abilities. While objectively sound, I mustn’t be remiss when considering less personality and emotion-based variables. Being respectful and seeing potential can be just as rewarding as being cold and calculating.
