What is your interest in IT? When did your interest in IT start?

I’m interested in learning to create and maintain. Currently, I’m most attracted to learning to code and eventually program. Varying different languages that directly impact and affect our lives, because my interest lies within technologies enabling potential. I believe that technology has the potential to serve the betterment of humanity. It’s helped us build cities, rockets and structures that sit on the moon. It’s allowed us to cultivate economies, and artists, giving dreamers a voice and a medium to express their ideas. Most recently, and recognisable is its capacity to keep us connected through the hardest, and most trying of times. Namely COVID-10. Now while a world connected and supported is breathtakingly beautiful; it’s also hauntingly dangerous.

My interest in IT started in my adolescence. Like the majority of Gen Z, I had the privilege of growing up with a computer in my pockets, tenfold more powerful than the original Turing Machine used to decode Nazi transmissions or the IBM 7090s used by NASA during the Space Race in the 60s. And despite my other youthful passions, technology remained a constant, yet ever-changing variable, enabling me, and others like me. I wasn’t born with an iPad in my stroller, but I did drool over the opportunistic “Computer Classes” we had in Primary School in the late 2000s, and early 2010s. I have fond memories of using the classic bitmap drawer ‘KidPix’, or playing the very first builds, and on the pioneering servers of Minecraft. I started with the classic Nokia 3310, playing snake. Couple that with an iPod Shuffle 2nd Gen, and I was good to go. Till that fateful day, when I got my first hand-me-down iPhone 5 from my dad. I cherished that, and still have it tucked away to this day.

Technology and the role it plays in our everyday lives is so overt, it’s covert. On the surface, we use smartphones for everything, and our perception of the computer in our pockets is white-washed by all the social media, and streaming services we’re constantly plugged into. But rarely do we ever wonder what it takes to maintain and upkeep such an essential part of our lives. That’s why I’m interested in IT. All too often I found myself stopping and wondering how everything is interconnected. What it takes to make something as borderline comical as a smart-watch function. What prompted the introduction of first-person shooters into the gaming market? And where on God’s green Earth did it all come from?


Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest?

Narrowing down a particular event or person that sparked my interest in IT is hard. Growing up I had a general interest, built PCs, took IT electives, learn rudimentary forms of web development etc. However, if I were to narrow it down to something more recent, it would probably be a chain of events starting during the 2021 Sydney Lockdown. I applied and was successful in taking up a position in Apple’s retail stores. Initial in sales, I eventually moved to the Genius Bar as a technician. Pair that with Meta’s “Connect 2021” event and unveiling of their plans surrounding AR and VR technology. IT sprung to the forefront of my mind. AR especially has very promising commercial and well as industry-based applications. In an environment where people were isolated from each other, the pandemic showed that working from home was not only possible but, in some areas, beneficial. All ideas have rocky territory to navigate, but the concept of the “Metaverse”; a digital environment, utilising and implementing various forms of technology to aid in the crucial, social and collaborative “work-from-home” dilemma, is a promising step in the right direction. Companies like Meta aren’t the only ones pioneering ground-breaking concepts and technology. I don’t know where I’m going to fit into it, but I’m excited to see where this will take me.

Why did you choose RMIT?

With a rapidly changing, and dare I say, evolving means by to learn and enter the workforce/job, RMIT’s online study format, via Open Universities Australia, offers the flexibility and range I’m in the market for. Balancing working, studying and a healthy home/social life is no mean feat. Upon doing research, and speaking with student support advisors, friends and colleagues who have undergone, or are continuing to undergo online study, RMIT seemed like the logical choice.

My larger degree, at this point in time, is the Bachelor of Information Technology, however, I do have a growing interest in coding, and programming. I’m fascinated by the prospect of backend development, and generally the skill it takes to build and maintain such large-scale systems.
