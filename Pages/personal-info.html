About Me

Hello, I'm Joshua or Josh. I don't really have a preference. I am Filipino/Australian and grew up on the Northern Beaches of Sydney. I'm the eldest of 3 to 2 cheeky parents, and I have two gorgeous dogs. “Buddy", a 9-year-old Purebred Cattle Dog, and “Skatter”, a 2-and-a-half-year-old Pomeranian cross Maltese.

I work "around" the tech-based industry, less so "in it". I've always had a very active interest in coding, and I am in love with the progression of technology and its potential for humanity. Despite all the negative variables, I prefer to focus on the sheer innovation and imagination opportunities it can provide. Technology for the betterment of humankind, as it were. As for goals surrounding my studies. I'm interested in the field of Cyber Security or some variance of development, but I haven't quite narrowed that down yet.

Outside the course criteria, my personal interests include films, music, comics, and video games. Specifically, I really enjoy watching and dissecting all manner of films. Some of my all-time favourite directors are Wes Anderson, Edgar Wright, Zac Snyder, Steven Spielberg, and Quentin Tarantino. Most of the movies I love are remarkably stylised and are true testaments to what can be achieved when you look at it from a different perspective. I'm also a musician; I primarily play the guitar, but once upon a time, I played the piano and bass and sang, all at a relatively high proficiency. Nowadays, I casually grace the shower and my dogs with the sound of my voice and guitar.

I love varying comic and graphic novel types; however, my favourite "genre", as it is, is superheroes. I grew up reading about and watching them. Like any hectic fan, I have firm opinions on different topics and characters; yes, saying something "controversial" will trigger a tick in my brain. I may feel compelled to argue either for or against it. Finally, I play video games quite often. It’s a part of my weekly routine. Nowadays, I use it to destress, although, at times, it tends to have the opposite effect. But I have played my fair share of vintage to popular to obscure single-player/co-op campaign and multiplayers of varying genres, including sandboxes, multiplayer online battle arenas (M.O.A.B.), real-time strategies (R.T.S.), role-playing games (RPG), simulators, action-adventure and finally first and third person shooters, all whilst growing up. I loved most of them. Like so many other things, they represent different stages in my life, and I'm fond of nearly all of them for one reason or another. Except for "Battlefield 2042".

It's worth mentioning that I am wildly introverted and prefer the company of the few over the many. I've struggled with social anxiety my entire life, but more recently, I've begun to take hold. I do socialise regularly, as required by my job. It's usually the way. I work as a Genius Bar technician at the Apple Store in Sydney. As you can imagine, empathy and kindness are essential when supporting people needing help.



What is your interest in I.T.? When did your interest in I.T. start?

My interest lies within technologies enabling potential. I'm curious about learning to create and maintain. Currently, I'm most attracted to learning to code and eventually program in varying different languages that directly impact and affect our lives. I believe that technology has the potential to serve the betterment of humanity. It's helped us build cities, rockets and structures that sit on the moon. It's allowed us to cultivate economies, and artists, giving dreamers a voice and a medium to express their ideas. Most recent and recognisably is its capacity to keep us connected through the most challenging and trying times. Namely COVID-19. Now while a world connected and supported is breathtakingly beautiful, it's also hauntingly dangerous.

My interest in I.T. started in my adolescence. Like the majority of Gen Z, I had the privilege of growing up with a computer in my pockets, tenfold more potent than the original Turing Machine used to decode Nazi transmissions or the I.B.M. 7090s used by NASA during the Space Race in the 60s. And despite my other youthful passions, technology remained a constant yet ever-changing variable, enabling me and others like me. I wasn't born with an iPad in my stroller, but I did drool over the opportunistic "Computer Classes" we had in Primary School in the late 2000s and early 2010s. I have fond memories of using the classic bitmap drawer 'KidPix' and playing the very first builds on the pioneering servers, no less, of Minecraft. I started with the classic Nokia 3310, playing snake. Couple that with an iPod Shuffle 2nd Gen, and I was good to go. Until that fateful day, I got my first hand-me-down iPhone 5 from my dad. I cherished that and still have it tucked away to this day.

Technology and its role in our everyday lives is "so overt, it's covert". On the surface, we use smartphones for everything. Our perception of the computer in our pockets is whitewashed by the copious amounts of social media and streaming services we're constantly plugged into. But rarely do we wonder what it takes to maintain and upkeep such an essential part of our lives. That’s why I'm interested in I.T. All too often, I found myself stopping and wondering how everything is interconnected. What it takes to make something as borderline comical as a smart-watch function. What prompted the introduction of first-person shooters into the gaming market? And where on God’s green Earth did it all come from?


Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest?

Narrowing down a particular event or person who sparked my interest in I.T. isn't particularly easy. Growing up, I had a general interest, built P.C.s, took I.T. electives, learned rudimentary forms of web development etc. However, if I were to narrow it down to something more recent, it would probably be a chain of events starting during the 2021 Sydney Lockdown. I applied and successfully took up a position in Apple's retail stores. Initial in sales, I eventually moved to the Genius Bar as a technician. Pair that with Meta's "Connect 2021" event and unveiling of their plans surrounding A.R. and V.R. technology. I.T. sprung to the forefront of my mind. A.R. especially has very encouraging commercial and well as industry-based applications. In an environment where people were isolated from each other, the pandemic showed that working from home was not only possible but, in some areas, beneficial. All ideas have rocky territory to navigate, but the concept of the "Metaverse", a digital environment utilising and implementing various forms of technology to aid in the crucial, social and collaborative "work-from-home" dilemma, is a promising step in the right direction. Companies like Meta aren't the only ones pioneering ground-breaking concepts and technology. I don't know where I will fit into it, but I'm excited to see where this will take me.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

With a rapidly changing, and dare I say, evolving means by which to learn and enter the workforce/job, RMIT's online study format, via Open Universities Australia, offers the flexibility and range I'm in the market for. Balancing working, studying, and a healthy home/social life is no mean feat. Upon doing research, and speaking with student support advisors, friends and colleagues who have undergone or are continuing to undergo online study, RMIT seemed the logical choice.

My larger degree, at this point in time, is the Bachelor of Information Technology; however, I do have a growing interest in coding and programming. I'm fascinated by the prospect of backend development and the skill it takes to build and maintain such large-scale systems.
