Software Engineer - Apple Australia


A description (in your own words) of the position, and particularly what makes this position appealing to you.

The position requires you to execute and maintain a high quality of code and, by extension, security, integrity and performance surrounding specific applications. Utilising the likes of CI/CD, GitHub, and Jenkins as tools. Debugging and troubleshooting abilities are a requirement, and the need to meet deadlines is a vital aspect of the job. A desire to learn and excel is a must. And with that comes responsibility and personal pride in your work.

The most appealing part of this job, aside from an opportunity to work on projects used by the public day in and out, is the value placed on responsibility and integrity. Fostering a healthy environment that promotes transparent and fair collaboration can often sound like a pipe dream to some. But the level of innovation that Apple is working on and its relevant presence in today’s economy means that you're held accountable. Not only for any shortcomings that may arise but for all of the wins. The element of uncertainty, mixed with pressure, whilst striving to keep an equilibrium of sorts, means that you'd be challenged every day you step off the lift. What’s most reassuring is Apple’s commitment to equal opportunity. Regardless of race, religion, gender, or political standpoint. Everyone is guaranteed the same opportunity as those. And are treated as such. Makes for a very unique point of view when attacking issues head-on.


A description (in your own words) of the skills, qualifications and experience required for the position.

Proven capacity in Java/Java E.E. and front and backend services ranging from XML, JSON, HTTP and SSL to JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and all-encompassing J.S. frameworks. Additionally, experience and competencies in S.F.D.C. Apex, Visualforce, Lightning web components, relational databases, data modelling, object-oriented programming, and design patterns. Development environments like agile and scrum will also be in play.


A description (in your own words) of the skills, qualifications and experience you currently have.

My current capacity surrounding the requirements for this role is truthfully limited. I'm familiar with JavaScript, HTML, and CSS and have rudimentary knowledge and experience with XML, JSON, and HTTP. However, that is currently the extent of my ability.

Other relevant information, although relative, includes my being an Apple Certified Technician, having been through extensive training as an employee for Apple's base U.I. and O.S., and gaining familiarity and the ability to perform repairs on their hardware. I also have experience as a live audio engineer/technician, broadcast technician, film set gaffer, camera operator, camera control unit, exposure operator, and 2nd A.C., All of these, have contributed to skills surrounding critical assessment and creative and logical problem-solving.


A plan describing how you will obtain the skills, qualifications and experience required for the position, building on those you have now. This need not be greatly detailed (and will probably change significantly over time anyway) but try to be as specific as you can.

Starting with this undergraduate degree in Information Technology, I plan to take my knowledge and investigate additional studies surrounding Cyber Security and general Computer Sciences.

Whilst studying, I have already begun to teach myself coding languages, starting with “back to basics” front-end languages such as JavaScript, HTML and CSS. With plans to sequentially and strategically learn more due to my genuine interest surrounding the field. Reinforcing my foundations is a strategy I'm employing because I want to be confident in my ability. I believe that to demonstrate my impending expertise, I'll need to first be confident in my capabilities.

I also plan to build a portfolio and develop projects close to my heart. The likes of which you'll be able to review in the "Project Ideas" section of this site.
